RAILWAY FACILITY


Arunachal Pradesh got its first railway line in late 2013 with the opening of the new link line from Harmuti on the main Rangpara North-Murkongselak railway line to Naharlagun in Arunachal Pradesh. The state capital Itanagar was added to the Indian railway map on 12 April 2014 via the newly built 20 kilometre Harmuti-Naharlagun railway line, when a train from Dekargaon in Assam reached Naharlagun railway station, 10 kilometres from the centre of Itanagar, a total distance of 181 kilometres.



AIR TRANSPORTATION


Itanagar Airport, a Greenfield project serving Itanagar is being planned at Holongi. The existing state owned Daporijo Airport, Ziro Airport, Along Airport, Tezu Airport and Pasighat Airport are small and are not in operation. The government has proposed to operationalize these airports. Before the state was connected by roads, these airstrips were originally used for the transportation of food.



MEDICAL FACILITY


In Arunachal Pradesh, the population density here is very less. Because of which healthcare delivery cost is very high. There is no any private medical college till now. Arunachal Pradesh is lacking super specialties. Also, state is facing shortage of doctors such as neurosurgeon, cardiologist. The state is still very far behind the Indian healthcare scenario.
